Electronic Chessboard Project

With the current Covid-19 pandemic, this group project challenged myself and my co-developer Christofer Tavano. We set out to create a prototype electronic chessboard that would enable players to engage in a game of chess with a real-world opponent without the opponent present in the room. In addition, this chessboard has the capability to act as great tool for beginners to educate the mechanics of each chess piece.

The idea of this project came to me one night while I was playing chess on a mobile application. As I was getting punished by an experienced player, I concluded that playing on a mobile app does not compare to the same nostalgic feeling of playing on a physical chessboard. I realized that people no longer sit down together and play chess with one another. So, we hope to resolve this issue by creating a chessboard that someone could have on display in their office or living room. A player could complete their turn during their morning coffee, go about their day and return to the game when they are available again. We believe this is a unique way to maintain relationships and would be a product I would personally purchase.

We chose to use a Raspberry Pi 3 to serve as the microcontroller in this project. And after spending a couple of months working with the Pi 3, we will certainly use it again in future projects. The most prominent language that we have been using for the duration of the Computer Engineering program was C#. At this point in time we elected it necessary for a change. We needed a language that was going to be more suitable. Ultimately, we decided on Python. Neither one of us had prior experience with Python, so it was refreshing to indulge in a new language.

Given the current circumstances of Covid-19, unfortunately we had to put this project on pause. After society returns to a more stable and safer environment, we hope to return to this project. We would like to create a second iteration of our concept and further improve on our design.
